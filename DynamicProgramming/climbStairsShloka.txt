1. Base Case

यदा सोपानं n द्वौ वा, तन्न्यूनं भवेत् ।
n एव मार्ग-संख्या, तत् ज्ञेयं आद्य-कारणम् ॥

IAST:
yadā sopānaṁ n dvau vā, tan-nyūnaṁ bhavet |
n eva mārga-saṅkhyā, tat jñeyaṁ ādya-kāraṇam ||

English transliteration (phonetic):
yadā sopānam n dvau vā, tannyūnam bhavet |
n eva mārgasaṅkhyā, tat jñeyam ādyakāraṇam ||

Translation:
When the staircase has only n = 1 or 2 steps,
less than that cannot exist.
Then n itself is the number of ways—
this should be known as the base case (the initial cause).



2. The Memoization

पश्य स्मृतिकोशं t[n] यत्नात्, ऋण-एकं न चेत् भवेत् ।
पूर्वं गणितं तत् फलं गृहाण, वृथा श्रमं मा कुरु ॥

IAST:
paśya smṛti-kośaṁ t[n] yatnāt, ṛṇa-ekaṁ na cet bhavet |
pūrvaṁ gaṇitaṁ tat phalaṁ gṛhāṇa, vṛthā śramaṁ mā kuru ||

English transliteration (phonetic):
pashya smritikoshaṁ t[n] yatnāt, ṛṇaekaṁ na chet bhavet |
pūrvam gaṇitam tat phalam gṛhāṇa, vrithā shramaṁ mā kuru ||

Translation:
Look carefully into the memory table t[n];
if its value is not -1 (uncomputed),
take the previously computed result—
do not waste effort in recalculating.


3. The Recursive Step

n−1 सोपान-मार्गाः, n−2 मार्गैः च योजयेत् ।
तत् योगं t[n] कोशे संरक्ष्य, तदेव प्रतिपादयेत् ॥

IAST:
n−1 sopāna-mārgāḥ, n−2 mārgaiḥ ca yojayet |
tat yogaṁ t[n] kośe saṁrakṣya, tadeva pratipādayet ||

English transliteration (phonetic):
n−1 sopānam-mārgāḥ, n−2 mārgaiḥ cha yojayet |
tat yogam t[n] koshe samrakṣya, tadeva pratipadayet ||

Translation:
Combine the paths from (n−1) steps
and from (n−2) steps together;
store that sum into the memory table t[n],
and return that as the final result.
